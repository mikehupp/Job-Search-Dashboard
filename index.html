Comment Bank

Adapt these to make substantive comments. Never post "Great insight" or "Thanks for sharing."

Customer Success Posts
CS / Retention
Strong point. Customer success tends to become much easier when teams focus not just on responsiveness, but on proactively reducing uncertainty for the client. Most churn isn't from bad service — it's from silent anxiety.
CS / Onboarding
This is often underestimated. A lot of long-term account health is shaped during onboarding, because that's where confidence and expectations are formed. If that phase is noisy, trust gets harder to rebuild later.
CS / Value
Agreed. The shift from relationship management to value delivery is where most CS teams get stuck. It's easy to be liked. It's harder to be the person clients credit for their outcomes.
Delivery & Project Leadership Posts
Delivery
This is a strong point. In delivery settings, trust usually improves when teams move beyond status updates and start surfacing risks, dependencies, and decisions more clearly. That usually changes the conversation with clients fast.
Alignment
Agreed. A lot of client friction comes less from execution itself and more from unclear ownership and communication gaps between teams. Better internal clarity often solves what looks like a client issue.
Cross-func
I've seen this play out in cross-functional software projects. Alignment improves fast when client expectations, internal timelines, and escalation paths are all made visible early — not at the point of crisis.
Retention
This also connects strongly to retention. Clients are often more forgiving of delays than they are of uncertainty. The teams that over-communicate risk end up with far fewer escalations.
Stakeholder & Communication Posts
Stakeholders
Yes — communication quality changes outcomes. In many cases, better visibility into risks and decisions matters more than simply increasing update frequency. Less noise, more signal.
Remote teams
I've seen this too. Remote collaboration works much better when ownership and decision paths are explicit, not assumed. Async-first environments punish ambiguity more harshly than co-located ones.
Leadership
This is underrated. People talk about execution but the teams I've seen move fastest had almost no surprises internally — they'd already done the alignment work before anything went out the door.
TAM / Technical Account Posts
TAM
Strong framing. The best TAMs I've seen operate more like internal advocates for the client — they close gaps before the client even names them. That's where the expansion conversations come naturally.
SaaS / Product
Agreed. Product adoption often isn't a product problem — it's a communication and enablement problem. The teams that close that gap early see far better retention numbers.

Operating Rules

Your non-negotiables. Read these when you're tempted to cut corners.

Connection Rules
✅
Filter before adding. Ask: Is this person in my target function, company, or hiring chain? Could familiarity realistically create opportunity? Would I actually want a conversation from this person?
✅
Default: connect without a note. Most notes are wasted and feel forced. Only add a note when you have real context — shared company, mutual colleague, their active job post.
🚫
Never connect randomly. Do not add other job seekers, random people, or anyone you'd never realistically want a conversation with. Relevance over volume.
Engagement Rules
✅
Comments must add value. Add perspective, show judgment, connect to real experience, or extend the discussion. 1–2 sentences minimum. Reads like a practitioner.
🚫
Never post "Great post," "Totally agree," or "Thanks for sharing." These are invisible. They signal no thought. They do nothing.
✅
24-hour rule. After connecting, check if they posted. Engage within 24 hours. After someone comments on your post, reciprocate. After someone views your profile and is relevant, engage their content.
Messaging Rules
✅
Only message with real context. You engaged their post. They viewed your profile. They accepted your request. They posted a relevant role. You have shared background.
🚫
Never send lazy asks. "Hi, I'm open to work." "Any roles for me?" "Let me know if you hear anything." These require the other person to do all the thinking. They center the interaction entirely around you.
🚫
Never attach your CV in a cold message. It signals desperation and creates friction before trust is established.
Posting Rules (2026 Algorithm)
✅
Default format: PDF carousel. Upload a multi-page PDF as your post. 5–7 slides. 6.6% avg engagement rate vs 2% for text. Same content, 3–4x reach.
✅
Post 8–10 AM PH time. Stay active 90 mins after. The golden hour determines whether LinkedIn expands your reach. Only 5% of posts that underperform in hour 1 recover.
🚫
Never put external links in post body. 60% reach penalty. Links to external sites (CV, job boards, company pages) belong in the first comment only — and even then expect some suppression.
🚫
Never post about your job search publicly. No rejection diary posts. No "I'm looking for work, please help." No documenting your application count. This makes you look distressed, not credible.
Mindset Rules
🎯
Networking supports applications — it does not replace them. If LinkedIn engagement starts feeling like an avoidance tactic, it's not working. The main engine is still: applications + positioning + interview conversion.
🎯
You are not trying to impress strangers. You are trying to make the right 20–30 people think: "I've seen this guy around. He seems solid." That is the entire game.
🎯
Familiarity compounds. Start before you feel ready. The system is slow at first and invisible. Recognition builds underneath. Then one day a message lands. Stay consistent through the quiet period.

Key Algorithm Rules
⚡
Golden Hour: The first 60–90 minutes after posting determines your reach. Only 5% of posts that underperform in hour 1 recover. Post at 8–10 AM PH and respond to every early comment immediately.
🚫
External links = 60% reach penalty. Any post with a link to an external site gets suppressed. Put links in the first comment only if absolutely necessary. Keep your post body clean.
💬
Comment quality > likes. LinkedIn now detects whether comments meaningfully contribute to the topic. A post with 50 real comments outperforms a post with 500 likes. Your engagement on others' posts also matters — AI-generated comments get 5x fewer replies.
📄
PDF carousels create dwell time. Each page swipe signals engagement to the algorithm. They allow you to tell a complete story, break down a concept, and demonstrate expertise. Create carousels in Canva — use their LinkedIn carousel templates.
🏠
Company pages are dying. Company pages get just 5% of feed allocation. Personal profiles dominate 65% of content. Your personal posts reach 561% more people than reshares from company pages. Always post from your personal profile.
#️⃣
Use 3–5 specific hashtags max. Hashtags play a smaller role now — LinkedIn uses improved text detection. Too many hashtags (7+) correlate with 68% reduced reach. Use 3–5 niche-specific tags, not generic ones.
📅
Best posting days: Tue–Thu. Mid-morning (8–11 AM) in your local timezone. Weekend posts get less immediate engagement but continue gaining traction into Monday. Avoid Sunday evening posts for APAC audiences.
🔄
Behavioral reinforcement loop. When you consistently interact with someone's content, LinkedIn shows you more of their posts — and them more of yours. Your names start appearing in each other's feeds. This is the familiarity engine. It compounds over 2–4 weeks of consistent activity.
Profile Search Optimization
🔍
Recruiters search like Google. They type exact titles: "Customer Success Manager" or "Technical Account Manager" — not variations. Use the exact title everywhere: headline, about, experience. Not "client success leader" or "account growth driver."
🛠️
Skills section = 40% of recruiter searches now start here. LinkedIn increased max skills from 50 to 100. Add skills to every past role, not just your current one. Treat skills like SEO keywords — if you've done it, list it.
📋
Upload your CV to LinkedIn Jobs. Go to Jobs → Résumés and Application Data → upload → toggle on "Share résumé data with hirers." This lets employers find you in backend searches beyond just your headline. Master CV is fine for this — you're not chasing a specific role, you're creating inbound.
